Project：PISA¶

The background of the information we are going to analyze this time is as follows:

The Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) is a worldwide study by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) in member and non-member nations intended to evaluate educational systems by measuring 15-year-old school pupils' scholastic performance on mathematics, science, and reading.[1] It was first performed in 2000 and then repeated every three years. Its aim is to provide comparable data with a view to enabling countries to improve their education policies and outcomes. It measures problem solving and cognition. [2]

reference: WiKi

Questiones¶

For this information, I am very interested in several aspects:

  1. According to [Wikipedia] (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_population), the world's most populous countries in 2010 are China, India, and the United States. Are these students participating in PISA also the majority? What?
  2. Does the student's parents have an impact on the student's learning score?
  3. The stereotype of gender, the number of boys is good, the girls are reading well, is this really true?
  4. Which countries are the strengths of which countries?

Data Wrangling¶

Due to the huge size of the data, the number of fields is more than 650. Therefore, we will do some processing on the original data according to the problem, as described below:

  1. Keep the fields related to the question to explore - country code - Gender - Is the parents at home? - Country of birth - Scores for each subject
  2. Finishing - According to [Country codes according to ISO-3166] (https://laendercode.net/en/), data of the same country but different names are integrated into the same country name. If it is not found, it is Unknown; The zone name is also summarized as Unknown - Rename the field according to the field description of pisa2012.csv - Category data is changed to a value of 0, 1 - Average scores for each subject
  3. Finally, we will save the organized data as pisa_data_clean.csv to explore the analysis.

# Here, we compute a count which shows the different countries for monter, father, and the student.
# So, 1 means all three are born in the same country
# 2 means two of them are born in different countries
# 3 means all of them are birn in different country
def count_distinct_countries(str):
    s = set()
    for t in str.split(","):
        s.add(t)
    return len(s)

# The three country names of mother, father, and the student are merged to one string for the later process
df_clean['Merged Birth Countries'] = df_clean['Birth Country of Mother'] + "," +  df_clean['Birth Country of Father'] + "," + df_clean['Birth Country'];

# we apply the previously defined function to the merged names of countries to compute the representing number of 
# different birth countries 
df_clean['Count Distinct Countries'] = df_clean['Merged Birth Countries'].apply(count_distinct_countries);

Conclusions¶

  • The performance of girls in reading is not only better than that of boys, but also different from the stereotypes of girls. The performance of girls in mathematics and science is no less than that of boys.
  • Students with a parent's company will have a better study.
  • The different national backgrounds of student families, the more prominent the scores for male students.
  • Finally, in the PISA's various subjects, in addition to reading, mathematics and science are the scoring strengths of Asian countries. However, each subject is closely related to each other. In general, the scores of each subject will not fall too much.
